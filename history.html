A Soton tradition

Starting as a 4th year group design project (GDP) project in 2012, the Lunar Hopper has seen continual development, and has been contributed to by over 20 of Southampton's engineering students.

The Hopper can trace it's origins back to a GDP project in 2012. This project's goal was to create a hybrid rocket engine capable of producing 40N of thrust. Since then 4 more GDPs have been involved in the Hopper project, all improving on the previous year's work.

From September 2016 the Lunar Hopper has been developed by members of the Student's Union Spaceflight Society, whose main task now is to prepare the Hopper for flight testing.
