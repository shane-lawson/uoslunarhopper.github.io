The UK's only student-built lunar lander, powered by the Uk's only student-built hybrid rocket motor.

The University of Southampton Lunar Hopper is a prototype Lunar exploration vehicle, designed, built and tested by students.

Based on the principle of verticle take-off, verticle landing (VTVL), the Hopper is designed to access areas of the Moon that a rover could not. It's mission is to land on the Lunar surface, perform scientific analysis, then take-off again to move to a different area of interest. It is designed to perform 3-5 of these "hops", hense the name.

UoS Lunar Hopper supervised by Prof. Tony Musker and Dr. Charlie Ryan
