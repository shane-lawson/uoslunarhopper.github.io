  • 400N Hybrid Rocket Motor

    Built by the Phase III team, the 400N hybrid rocket motor is the powerhouse of the Lunar Hopper. It is a hybrid motor, meaning it burns both solid and liquid components.

    The solid component is fuel grain, formed into hollow cylinders in the main body of the rocket. The liquid is high-test peroxide, acting as the oxidiser for the motor.

    Togeather the two are able to produce 400N of thrust for the Hopper, allowing it to fulfil it's mission.

  • Attitude Control System

    Keeping the Hopper steady during flight is one of the key challenges of the project. The attitude control system (ACS) was started by Phase VI, and is a reactionary control system.

    Gyroscopes detect the Hopper's attitude, and if it begins to tilt off-course, the ACS system will fire one of the four thrusters at the top of the Hopper, pushing the craft in the oposite direction

    The system uses inert nitrogen gas as the propellant for the trusters.

    These high-pressure tanks hold the oxidiser used by the rocket motor, HTP, at a safe pressure.

  • N2Nitrogen Gas Tanks

    The nitrogen in these tanks is used by the ACS system to keep the craft stable, and also by the rocket motor as a catalyst.
